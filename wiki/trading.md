# Trading

Trading is one of the most reliable ways to build wealth in Pirate's Quest.

Each port has its own player-driven economy. Prices change from port to port, so profit comes from moving goods intelligently instead of selling everything at the first harbor you see.

## The Basic Trade Loop

1. Find a good with a low buy price.
2. Sail to another port.
3. Sell that same good where the price is better.
4. Repeat with bigger cargo and better planning.

## Why Trading Matters

Trading funds nearly everything else in the game:

- ship upgrades
- repairs
- cannonballs
- risky sea runs
- long-term progression

If you can make consistent profit, you can recover from losses and keep growing.

## Core Resources

Resources come from collection points around the map and from port markets.

| Resource | Role |
|---|---|
| Wood | Crafting, trading, repairs |
| Iron | Components, trading |
| Fish | Trading, crafting |
| Tea | Valuable trade good, crafting |
| Gold | Currency used to buy and sell at ports |
| Cannonballs | Ammunition consumed when firing cannons |

Gold is the primary measure of success. The leaderboard ranks players by their gold.

## Choosing What to Carry

Some goods are good trade cargo. Others matter because they help keep your ship running.

- **Wood** helps with repairs and crafting-related costs.
- **Iron** is important for components and ship upgrades.
- **Fish** has both trading and practical value.
- **Tea** is described as a valuable trade good and crafting material.
- **Cannonballs** do not make you rich, but running out of them can make you helpless.

## Smart Trading Habits

- Check multiple ports before committing to a route.
- Do not fill your hold with random cargo just because it is available.
- Plan for the return trip if you can.
- Keep enough resources to stay safe instead of spending everything on profit.

## Distance And Profit

Nearby trades can still make money, but the best margins usually come from longer sails.

- Short routes are safer and faster.
- Long routes usually pay better.
- Expensive cargo is more painful to lose if another player catches you.

That tradeoff is the point. A short hop can keep you afloat. A long haul is how you build a real bankroll.

## Simple Route Examples

- Buy `Wood` in western ports, then look for better wood prices in the east.
- Buy `Iron` where mining is common, then carry it to remote ports that depend on imports.
- Buy `Fish` in fishing towns, then sell it farther inland or across the map.
- Buy `Tea` near Tidefall or Krakenfall, then bring it back to western or southern ports.

## Trading and Progression

The early quests push you to trade on purpose. One of the main lessons is learning to buy core goods, sell them for profit, and build up a supply of gold.

That is the basic money-making pattern:

- learn prices
- make repeatable money
- use that money to improve your ship
