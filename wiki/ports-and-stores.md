# Ports and Stores

Ports are the main hubs of Pirate's Quest. They are where you regroup, trade, repair, and plan your next trip.

## Ports Are Safe Zones

You cannot take damage while docked at a port. That makes ports the best place to recover after a difficult run and the safest place to make decisions about money, cargo, and upgrades.

## What You Can Do At a Port

Each port is unique, but ports can offer these services:

- **Shop**: buy and sell resources using the local economy
- **Characters**: talk to NPCs who offer quests and world information
- **Crew**: hire crew members at certain ports
- **Components**: buy and equip ship upgrades
- **Healing**: repair your ship using resources
- **Vault**: build personal storage that survives death

## The Shop

The shop is where the trading loop happens.

- Buy goods where they are cheap.
- Sell them where prices are better.
- Use profits to fund repairs, upgrades, and your next trip.

Because each port's economy is different, the shop is not just a convenience menu. It is one of the main gameplay systems.

## Characters and Information

Characters in port can offer quests, useful hints, and new progression steps.

## Healing and Repairs

The sea wears ships down. Ports let you repair before the next fight or trip.

Repairing is part of the normal loop, not a sign that you failed. If your hull is low, fixing it before leaving port is usually the smart move.

## Vault Use

The vault is the answer to one of the game's biggest risks: losing valuable cargo when you die.

If you are about to do something dangerous, a port is where you decide whether to bank resources and gold first.

## Good Port Habits

- Repair before starting a risky run.
- Sell cargo you no longer need.
- Restock useful supplies.
- Check for quest opportunities.
- Store valuables before chasing danger.
