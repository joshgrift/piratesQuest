# Getting Started

Your ship is everything. It is how you move, trade, fight, and survive.

If you are just starting, focus on learning the sea before worrying about perfect efficiency.

## Your First Priorities

### 1. Learn to move the ship

Start by getting comfortable steering, slowing down, and lining up your approach to ports.

### 2. Look around while sailing

The sea is dangerous when you stare straight ahead. Use the camera to check your surroundings and avoid drifting into trouble.

### 3. Dock at a port

Ports are your safe zones. Docking opens the port UI and gives you access to trading, quests, repairs, and other progression systems.

### 4. Start the early quest chain

The starter quests teach the basics one system at a time.

Early quests unlock:

- selling goods
- tavern conversations
- buying goods
- ship components
- ship class upgrades
- the vault

## What Ports Are For

When you dock, think of the port as your reset point between sea runs.

Ports let you:

- buy and sell goods
- talk to characters
- hire crew
- purchase components
- repair your ship
- build or use a vault

## A Safe Beginner Routine

Try this loop for your first few sessions:

1. Sail to a port and dock.
2. Check what that port buys and sells.
3. Accept a quest if one is available.
4. Leave port with a simple goal like trading one good or reaching another harbor.
5. Return to safety before your ship is in bad shape.

## Beginner Tips

- Do not leave port with no cannonballs.
- Do not stay at sea with a badly damaged hull if you can avoid it.
- Do not carry valuables you are not willing to lose.
- Use ports often. They are part of the loop, not a pause from it.
